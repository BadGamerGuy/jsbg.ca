Overview

Apologies is best with 4 players and these rules are written with teams in mind. With fewer players, skip the empty player sections of the board and ignore the team rules. For 2 players, use a single board instead of both.

Gameplay (Goal & Movement)

Start on your color’s start, go with the arrows around the board, and finish in your color’s finish. Only matching color pieces can enter a color’s finish. Unless a special card says otherwise, you always move forward.

Win condition: Once your pieces are all in your finish, you play your teammate’s pieces. If both teams’ pieces are fully finished, you win.

Dealing

The deal proceeds clockwise. Each dealer deals the entire deck over three hands: two hands of 4 cards and one hand of 5 cards. The dealer chooses which hand is which. After three hands, the whole deck has been dealt; the next player becomes dealer and repeats the three‑hand cycle.

Rule Priority

If rules conflict, prioritize in this order. House rules are always welcome—have fun.

  1. Follow higher‑order rules first when conflicts arise.
  2. Once in your finish, a piece cannot leave and you cannot pass pieces already in your finish.
  3. If you can play, you must—even if it hurts your position.
  4. Passing an opponent does nothing unless done with a King (see Special Cards).
  5. If you can’t play, discard your entire hand face‑up (fanned) so everyone knows which cards are now unavailable.
  6. If you discard a full hand, you may move one piece forward 1 space (e.g., Start 4 → Start 3).
  7. Once all of your pieces are in your finish, discard what remains in your hand.
  8. Landing on another player’s piece sends that piece home.
  9. You cannot land on your own piece. If your only legal move lands on your own piece, you’re considered unable to play and must discard.
  10. You can land on a teammate’s piece; doing so sends them back to their start.
  11. When sent back to start, a piece goes to the furthest available start space (e.g., if Start 3 is open, it goes there rather than Start 1).
  12. After your finish is complete, you begin playing your teammate’s pieces.
  13. If both your and your teammate’s pieces are all in finish, you win.
Optional team swap: At the start of each hand, teammates may swap one card face‑down. Recommended once you know the strategy—it speeds up the game.

Core Rules (Quick Reference)

  • Start/Finish are color‑matched; follow arrows around the board.
  • You must play if you legally can. Otherwise, discard face‑up.
  • Landing on an opponent sends them home. You can’t land on yourself. You can land on a teammate (sends them to start).
  • When returning to start, go to the furthest available start position.
  • After you finish your pieces, play your teammate’s pieces to help them finish.

Special Cards

Any card can be played as its face value (Ace=1, Jack=11, King=13) instead of its special ability. Board icons are reminders—no need to announce your hand.

Ace or King Leave Start

Either card can move a piece out of your start from any start slot (e.g., Start 3 → track).

4 Move Backward

May move backward as needed, enabling tactical retreats or set‑ups.

7 Split Movement

Split the 7 across any of your pieces on the board in any combination (e.g., 3+2+2).

Jack Swap Pieces

Swap your piece with any other player’s piece on the board.

King Pass & Send Home

In addition to leaving start, if you pass an opposing piece while moving with a King, you may send that piece back to start.
